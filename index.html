Hi there!

After obtaining a Bachelor of Business Administration in Finance, I wasn't sure where life was going to take me. I worked my way up to the title of manager in the service industry, but still, I yearned for a new challenge. As time went on, I considered my options: pursuing my master's degree, finding a new job, or changing careers altogether. Eventually, after lamenting over my dilemma with a close friend, he asked, "Have you ever considered coding?" I cocked an eyebrow quizzically, "Don't you need a crazy amount of school for that?" I asked. He then proceeded to explain how diverse coding has become, how different backgrounds are constantly welcome and, most importantly, he gave me all the resources I could ask.

That was the day I became curious about this whole "coding thing". At the beginning of 2016, I barely knew the difference between front end and back end development. Thanks to heaps of meet-ups, in-person and online courses, web searches, and the never-ending patience of my coding friends, I gained knowledge of programming and was ready to take it to another level.

I started down a path which was notorious for its highs and lows. I resigned myself to a future of frustration being followed by victorious laughter. I signed up for a Coding Bootcamp and graduated from DigitalCrafts' full-time immersive coding bootcamp January of 2018. I've been working daily to continue to improve and constantly learn since.

It has been a wild ride. And you know what? I love it.

Featured Project

Movies With React

Movies With React is an app built using React, SVG, AWS and The Movie Database API. When you first land on the main page, it shows you a list of newly released movies. You may also use the search bar to find specific movies. When you click on a movie, it will give you various information about it such as the release date, the rating, genre, a brief summary, and the cast. I'm a big movie person so I wanted to be able to stay up to date with all the new releases and this app does exactly that. (Click image for GitHub & Live Demo)
